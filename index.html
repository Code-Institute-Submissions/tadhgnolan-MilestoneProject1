Learning on the go


A young girl in her 20s, was telling her friend that finally after a brief explanation from a coworker, she learned how to make a coffee and at least that day she kind of liked the job. She learned how to use the expresso coffee machine and to make the basics brews: expresso and americano.


Those are the easy ones, but then comes the milk and that's a different thing 'cause steaming the milk changes the texture and temperature of the liquid, you do it wrong and the result can be very frothy, or too hot... so it is not just a matter of putting the steamer into the milk jug. To teach how to make latte, capuccino and flat white, needs more supervision, patience and time.


After a short time training, the girl was left alone, taking customers and making coffees. She was a bit surprised by this fact not only because she felt she didn't have the expertise, but also because she had just started the day before. Anyhow, instead of complaining or talking to the coworker, she carried on, doing the best she could.

The puddle


A guy has been working for 2 years as assistant manager in a bar. One day he noticed a leak from one of the pipes. He reported the problem to the manager who said he would sort it out, but time passed by, water drops kept falling and the guy kept reminding him about it.


One day the assistant manager was getting things ready before opening time and when he finished going down the stairs he stepped in the puddle and slipped down onto his back. He tried to get up but he couldn’t. His phone was ok, so he rang the manager, told him what happened and the answer was: I’m busy, I cannot go now. The guy cleary stated the fact he couldn’t get up and described the intensity of the pain, but his boss didn’t change his mind.


The assistant manager called his girlfriend, while she was on the way, he crawled upstairs. She waited about 20 minutes outside 'till her boyfriend, on all fours, was able to open the door. The ambulance didn’t take too long. At the hospital the diagnosis was a severe injury. For 2 months the guy couldn’t walk normally, straighten his back, nor was he able to lift any weight.


When he returned to work he quit, sued the manager, won the case and compensation far greater than the cost of simply repairing the leak.

The milkshake fan


In an ice cream shop with all sorts of customers, a young man with a certain addiction to drugs, was a fan of milkshakes. More than once a week he was in front of the fridge trying the many ice cream flavours, it was hard for him to make up his mind, he liked them all.


He always paid, he never complained not even about the price, he was kind and respectful, nevertheless out of the 4 or 5 employees, only one used to take him because the other ones avoided him.

On one occasion he didn't have enough money and was worried because the ice cream flavour chosen was popular and there wasn’t much left. The girl reassured him. The young man left promising he would be back.


Half an hour later he was there, money in hand. She prepared the order, he thanked her and even offered a tip, she smiled and denied. He insisted but she convinced him by recommending him to save it for the next milkshake. He thanked her again “you are the best” said, then sipping his drink waved at her and left the shop with a big smile.

Smell the smoke


In a food market, a girl who works frying food requests a extractor fan because of the smoke, the fumes and every time while working there she ends up with strong headaches, itchy eyes and more often she suffers fatigue. The owner tells her not to worry because the issue will be resolved.


Weeks after, there's no sign of the fan extractor. The girl brings the matter up again to the owner and he excuses himself by blaming the technician who never showed up. A couple of weeks later, the girl goes to the manager who advises her not to talk about this with the owner and that from now on she would look after the issue.


The girl never asked again about it and eventually she quit.


After sometime other employees raised the problem about the smoke and requested a fan extractor without any success. What happened next was that one day, while working with customers queueing, a staff member, under 18 years of age, just fainted. For the young one’s mother it was too much to tolerate and she had words with the owner.


But the extractor fan wasn't installed, not even after what transpired.

A lift up


The place opened before 7:00 am and from that moment on the early birds pop in for breakfast. Most of the clients are regulars, the deli assistant doesn’t know all of them yet and still she gets a bit nervous when seeing a “new face”, like the one that just arrived asking for a baguette with butter and bacon, but instead of bacon the man said rashers. She didn’t catch it immediately, she apologized and the man said it again: RASHERRRS. The girl finished the order wishing a good day to the customer that walked away without any goodbye.


Once the rush hour ended, the manager came to the girl, saying the man had complained because she speaks lowly and English wasn’t her mother tongue. The girl was expecting the manager to give out to her but instead he backed her up, telling the customer they liked a multicultural environment. He added that despite her shyness she was a good worker and as everyone once did, she was also starting in a new place.


The girl thanked the manager, he smiled back telling her not to be afraid of raising her voice and to trust more in herself the same way they trusted her.
